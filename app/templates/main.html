Lexical density is the ratio of the number of lexical words (nouns, adjectives, verbs, and adverbs) to the total number of words in a text.

Number of possessives: possessives are forms that we use to talk about possessions and relationships between things and people, for example: mother's smile, the clothes of my father.

Number of coordinated phrases: only adjectives, adverbs, nouns and verb phrases can be coordinated, for example: She is beautiful and smart. He smiled and went away.

Number of auxilaries: have, do, be, can, should, must...

Average number of words before root: a root is a main verb of the sentence, for example: She is beautiful and smart. My mother went to help them.

Number of adjectival clauses: an adjectival clause is a clause that modifies nominal phrase (noun), for example: There are many online sites offering booking facilities.

Number of functional n-grams: functional n-grams are frequent organising collocations, for example: is totally different from, a large amount of.

Derivational level 3: the number of words with suffixes from (Bauer and Nation 1993), for example: -able (readable), -er (reader), -ish (freakish), -less (peerless), -ly (quickly).

Derivational level 4: the number of words with suffixes from (Bauer and Nation 1993), for example: -tion (attention), -ess (happiness), -ful (wonderful), -ism (realism), -ist (specialist), -ity (civility).

Derivational level 5: the number of words with suffixes from (Bauer and Nation 1993), for example: -age (percentage), -al (approval), -ally (idiotically), -an (Russian).

Derivational level 6: the number of words with suffixes from (Bauer and Nation 1993), for example: -ee (attendee), -ic (poetic), -ify (intensify).

Number of sentences: a sentence is a group of words that is separated from another such group by a full stop, a question mark, an exclamation mark, a quotation mark, or suspension points.

Number of clauses: a clause is a structure with a subject and a predicate, for example: I read a book, Lola danced. - 2 clauses.

Number of lemmas: I am fine - 3 lemmas (I, be, fine).

Number of infinitives: to read, to dance.

Noun variation is the ratio of the number of nouns to the total number of lexical words (nouns, adjectives, verbs, and adverbs) in a text.

Verb variation is the ratio of the number of verbs to the total number of lexical words (nouns, adjectives, verbs, and adverbs) in a text.

Lexical sophistication is the ratio of the number of advanced words to the total number of words.

Number of linking phrases: besides, however, in conclusion...

Average length of the sentence: the average number of words per sentence.

Number of gerunds: doing, smiling, running...

Number of verbs in past simple: smiled, looked, tried...

Verb sophistication is the ratio of the number of advanced verbs to the total number of words.
